A comparative analysis of fair prescription rules

August 2024

Project overview

  • Goal: Generate fair prescription rules for a given dataset
  • Focus: Balance utility and fairness in rule selection
  • Comparison: CauSumX vs Greedy algorithm approaches
  • Dataset: Stack Overflow Developer survey (so_countries_col_new.csv)

Key observations

  • Greedy achieves higher utility (106,282.14 vs 75,694.75)
  • Both algorithms provide similar coverage (Greedy: 94.49%, CauSumX: 94.44%)
  • CauSumX has slightly higher protected coverage (95.08% vs 93.98%)
  • Both algorithms focus on similar attributes (YearsCoding, Gender, SexualOrientation)
  • Execution time is lower for CauSumX (328.85s vs 578.38s)

Fairness comparison

  • CauSumX: Lower utility but slightly higher protected coverage
  • Greedy: Higher utility with marginally lower protected coverage
  • Both algorithms achieve high overall coverage (>94%)
  • Similar focus on demographic attributes in selected rules

Conclusions

  • Greedy: Higher utility, but slightly lower protected coverage
  • CauSumX: Lower utility, but better execution time and protected coverage
  • Both algorithms show good overall performance in terms of coverage
  • Trade-off between maximizing utility and ensuring fairness is evident
  • Choice depends on specific use case priorities (utility vs. fairness vs. execution time)
  • Further research needed to optimize fairness-utility balance
